# Stage 2: Domain Shifting & Sparring

**Goal:** Force the Core Tension onto distant semantic domains to surface non-obvious visual metaphors, then spar with the user until one breakthrough metaphor is locked. Breakthrough ideas come from *combinational* thought — the friction of mapping the brand's tension onto a domain it has nothing to do with is what produces an image no median generator would reach for.

## What This Stage Achieves

A recorded **`domain-shift-spar.md`** capturing the three explored directions and the **single, collaboratively approved breakthrough metaphor** — distant enough to be original, but provably tied to the brand's Core Tension and use case. This metaphor is the seed for the one direction in Stage 3.

## Step-by-Step Procedure

1. **Take the Core Tension** from Stage 1 as the input to map.
2. **Pick 3 highly distant domains** — never the brand's own industry, and never three flavors of the same territory. Good hunting grounds: *mycology (fungal networks), orbital mechanics, medieval horology, deep-sea hydrothermal ecosystems, brutalist architecture, cellular biology, cartography, glassblowing, tidal systems, lockpicking/mechanisms, origami, seismology.*
3. **Map the tension onto each domain's mechanics** — not its surface look. Find where the domain *itself* embodies the same `A vs. B` opposition, and let that produce a concrete visual metaphor.
4. **Fit-check each metaphor out loud** (see heuristics). Discard any that's merely decorative or that smuggles a banned cliché back in; replace it with a stronger mapping before presenting.
5. **Present 3 verified directions** — each as `Domain → mechanic → visual metaphor → why it fits`.
6. **Spar (HITL).** Run a real creative Q&A, not a multiple-choice vote — drill into which aspects resonate and why, recombine, and push back on cliché drift, until **one** metaphor is locked.
7. **Record `domain-shift-spar.md`** (all three explored, the locked one starred with its rationale) and move to Stage 3.

## Domain-Shift Heuristics

- **Map the mechanic, not the mood.** Don't borrow a domain because it "feels cool" — borrow it because its internal mechanics *are* the brand's tension. Mycology earns its place for security because the network's protection logic mirrors *Hidden vs. Revealed*, not because mushrooms look interesting.
- **Distance is the point.** If a chosen domain is one step from the industry (a card game → casinos), it's too close; it will deliver the clichés Stage 1 just banned.
- **Three territories, not one idea in three outfits.** The directions must be genuinely different conceptual worlds.
- **Reject on forced fit.** If you have to strain to justify the mapping, it's decorative. Kill it and hunt again.

## Worked Mini-Example

> **Core Tension:** *Control vs. Chaos* (DECKHEADS — the curated deck vs. the unpredictable clash).
>
> **Direction A — Orbital mechanics.** *Mechanic:* a stable orbit is a knife-edge balance between a body's momentum (chaos, wanting to fly off) and gravity (control, pulling it in). *Metaphor:* the mark as two bodies locked in a tense elliptical orbit — order holding chaos in a deliberate, beautiful tension. *Fit:* directly embodies Control vs. Chaos; reads as premium and a little dangerous (one nudge and the orbit decays). Avoids every banned card cliché.
>
> **Direction B — Seismology.** *Mechanic:* tectonic strain builds invisibly (control) until it releases as a quake (chaos). *Metaphor:* the mark as a clean baseline that fractures into a controlled seismic spike. *Fit:* captures the "held, then unleashed" feel of revealing a deck — but risks reading as a generic audio-waveform; flag for sparring.
>
> **Direction C — Glassblowing.** *Mechanic:* molten chaos shaped by breath and steel into a fixed, fragile form (control). *Metaphor:* a mark that looks like cooling glass — fluid trapped mid-order. *Fit:* tactile and premium, but the "danger" reads as fragility, not threat — possibly off-tone.

## Sparring Heuristics (HITL)

- **Don't present-and-wait.** Lead with what you'd pick and why, then ask the user to attack it. Forge is a Devil's Advocate, not an order-taker.
- **Drill, don't poll.** "Does the orbit's *tension* matter more to you, or its *symmetry*?" beats "Which do you like?"
- **Catch cliché drift.** If the user pulls back toward a banned Matrix item, name it politely ("that's the card-fan we agreed to retire — how do we get its energy without the literal card?") and elevate instead of obeying.
- **Recombine freely.** The winner is often A's concept with B's energy. Synthesize, then confirm.
- **Headless mode:** auto-select the highest-fit metaphor, record the rationale, and proceed without pausing.

## Anti-Cliché Checklist (before locking)

- [ ] All three domains are genuinely distant from the brand's industry.
- [ ] Each metaphor maps a real *mechanic* of its domain, not just its surface look.
- [ ] No direction reintroduces an item from the Negative Constraint Matrix.
- [ ] The locked metaphor surprises a little, yet the user can articulate in one sentence exactly why it's right.

## What Good Looks Like

The three directions are genuinely distinct territories, not one idea in three outfits. Each survives its fit-check. The locked metaphor is distant enough to be original but the user can say precisely why it fits the brand's tension and use case.

## Done When

- Three verified `domain → mechanic → metaphor → fit` directions are recorded.
- Exactly one metaphor is explicitly approved by the user (or auto-selected in headless), with a one-sentence rationale.
- `domain-shift-spar.md` is written to the brand folder.

**Transition → Stage 3 (Basic Logo + Branding Mockup).** Hand the locked metaphor (and its rationale) to Stage 3 as the single seed for the one direction it renders. Keep the Negative Constraint Matrix attached — Stage 3 fit-checks the direction against it again.
